(1) Development

The supply chain starts in the development stage. In this stage, the game is going through the process of being developed. This stage is where ideas are discussed and put into code. All members of the team work on something during this stage.

(2) Production

The next step is production. In this step, the game is advertised and promotions are put on in order to gain popularity and anticipation. A few copies of the game may be released during this stage, but it is mainly focused on preparing for release and gaining popularity.
This is also the stage in which the developers would find a publisher. The publisher provides the developers with a platform on which to release their game.

(3) Online Retailer

In the online retailer step, the game is officially released to the public. Consumers can go wherever it is that you released the game and buy their own copy. Common online retailers include Steam and the Nintendo eShop.

This step is often identified as the "distribution" stage. It is in this stage of the supply chain that retail stores, such as Walmart or Target, will be selling your game as physical copies.

(4) End User

Finally, the last step of the supply chain is the end user stage. In this stage, the game has been bought and is ready to be played by the consumer.
